What A Co-Host Is

A trusted helper who helps the host read the room, fill the queue, and support pacing decisions.

What A Co-Host Is Not

Not a replacement host. Co-hosts guide, suggest, and help. The host still makes the final call.

Rule Of Thumb

Keep things moving, keep things clear, and only send signals that actually help the room right now.

What Co-Hosts Can Do

  • Build the queue early. Use the authenticated helper catalog on an approved iPad or laptop to search, browse, assign a singer, and add songs on someone’s behalf.
  • Fill out the night in advance. Add songs for upcoming singers so the room never feels thin or stalled.
  • Send fast audio notes to the host. In the Singer app, use Tell Host for Track Up, Track Down, Vocal Up, Vocal Down, or Mix Issue.
  • Help with guided decisions. When the host opens a co-host moment, co-hosts can vote on things like song face-offs or slot-fill choices.
  • Help protect pacing. Watch for thin stretches, open slots, or energy drops and help the host stay ahead of them.
Important: Audio notes are automatically tied to the current performance when one is live, so the host can see which song the note came from and how fresh it is.

What Co-Hosts Cannot Do

  • No direct mixer control. Co-hosts can signal audio issues, but they do not directly change track or vocal levels.
  • No forced queue control. Co-hosts do not directly reorder the whole queue or push a song live on their own.
  • No host override. Co-hosts do not replace the host on approvals, launches, scene changes, or final decisions.
  • No blind adds. Do not add a song unless you know exactly who it is for.
  • No signal spam. If you send the host an audio note, let them react before repeating it.

Best Practices For A Great Co-Host

  • Confirm the singer first. Before adding a song, make sure the guest, title, and artist are correct.
  • Think ahead, not just next. The best co-hosts keep the queue healthy 20 to 60 minutes out, not only one song ahead.
  • Use audio notes sparingly. Send a note when there is a real issue, not just a personal preference.
  • Keep the room feeling intentional. Help spot holes, open slots, and awkward pacing before the host has to scramble.
  • Escalate only when useful. If something is sensitive or unclear, talk to the host directly instead of guessing in the app.
