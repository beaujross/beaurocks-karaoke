Guest Journey

What the singer sees and what the room does around them

This is the sequence hosts and co-hosts should memorize because it doubles as the room script for the whole night: join fast, request fast, wait with confidence, then be ready when the room turns back toward you.

Step 1: Join the room

Guests should arrive on a streamlined join screen that feels event-branded but resolves directly into the live BeauRocks room. This is the fastest moment of the night, so keep it calm and direct.

  1. If the event Wi-Fi is posted, connect to it first.
  2. Scan the room QR or open app.beaurocks.app/?room=AAHF.
  3. Pick an emoji and enter the display name the host will recognize.

Step 2: Clear the room rules fast

This should feel like a quick consent step, not a second onboarding funnel. Guests need one calm checkpoint and one obvious continue button before the room opens up.

  1. Keep the copy short enough to scan in a few seconds.
  2. Use one checkbox and one clear CTA: Agree and Continue.
  3. Train staff to treat this as a quick yes-and-go moment.

Step 3: Search or browse for a song

Guests do not need to understand the backing-track system. They just need a clear request surface that feels inviting, current, and easy to explain during a busy rush.

  1. Lead with search for guests who already know their song.
  2. Keep browse available for casual guests who are deciding in the room.
  3. The first screen should point attention at Search + Add Song.
  4. Once a request is sent, the guest should understand that their phone becomes the queue status screen for the rest of the night.

Step 4: Watch the queue and stay ready

After a request is sent, the guest needs confidence that it landed and a simple way to understand how the room shifts between songs, applause, next-up, and celebration beats.

  1. Queue view should show order, wait context, and request status at a glance.
  2. Hosts can remind guests that moderated requests may wait for approval before going fully live.
  3. Guests should keep the phone handy once they are near the top.
  4. After songs, the room may briefly shift into applause, leaderboard, donation, or next-up moments before normal queue flow resumes.

What the room staff should say out loud

"Scan the QR, join room AAHF, pick your emoji and name, clear the quick rules, then search your song. After you send it, watch the queue on your phone and stay nearby when you get close."

  • Guests do not need a long app lesson.
  • The phone becomes their queue status screen for the night.
  • After songs, the room may pause for applause, donations, leaderboards, or next-up before the next singer starts.

Operations View

How the host runs the night without over-explaining it

The host and helper layers should feel coordinated, not duplicated. Helpers reduce friction; the host confirms, steers, and decides when the room shifts into special moments. The goal is a room that feels intentional, not busy.

Helper catalog

This is the approved co-host tool for queue building and guest rescue. It is the fastest way to help guests without handing them a second host dashboard.

  1. Select the singer first, then search or browse the song.
  2. Add songs on behalf of guests when the room needs help moving faster.
  3. Use Tell Host only for useful signals about audio, pacing, or guest issues.
  4. Keep this tool limited to trusted helpers on approved devices.

Host workspace

This is where the night stays coherent: live lane stays in view, the queue work stays organized, the inbox collects what needs attention, and the host chooses when to interrupt normal flow.

  1. Use the host workspace as the source of truth for timing, approvals, and room state.
  2. Switch between queue, add, inbox, and run of show from the same content rail.
  3. Promote trusted helpers from Audience: select the guest, tap Make Co-Host, and confirm the CO-HOST badge.
  4. Let helpers assist with adds and signals, but keep final control here.
  5. Use special moments intentionally: applause, next-up, leaderboard, donation, and support beats should punctuate the night rather than overwhelm it.

Phase 1: Doors open

Post one QR, repeat one join explanation, and keep the first guest action pointed at Songs. This is when confusion compounds, so simplicity matters most here.

Phase 2: Queue build

Let trusted helpers rescue stuck guests and build clean queue entries while you keep pacing, approvals, and stage readiness coherent.

Phase 3: Live room loop

Run songs in a steady rhythm. After each performance, the room may briefly shift into applause, donation, next-up, or leaderboard moments before the next singer starts.

Promote a co-host from Audience

  1. Open Audience.
  2. Click the guest in Lobby Lineup or on their audience card.
  3. Tap Make Co-Host.
  4. Look for the CO-HOST badge. Use Remove Co-Host to undo it.
