Guest Journey

What the singer sees from first scan to active queue

This is the sequence hosts and co-hosts should memorize, because this is the path guests will ask about all night.

Step 1: Join the room

Guests should arrive on a streamlined join screen that feels event-branded but resolves directly into the live BeauRocks room.

  1. If the event Wi-Fi is posted, connect to it first.
  2. Scan the room QR or open app.beaurocks.app/?room=AAHF.
  3. Pick an emoji and enter the display name the host will recognize.

Step 2: Clear the room rules fast

This should feel like a quick consent step, not a second onboarding funnel. Guests need one calm checkpoint and one obvious continue button.

  1. Keep the copy short enough to scan in a few seconds.
  2. Use one checkbox and one clear CTA: Agree and Continue.
  3. Train staff to treat this as a quick yes-and-go moment.

Step 3: Search or browse for a song

Guests do not need to understand the backing-track system. They just need a clear request surface that feels inviting and current.

  1. Lead with search for guests who already know their song.
  2. Keep browse available for casual guests who are deciding in the room.
  3. The first screen should point attention at Search + Add Song.

Step 4: Watch the queue and stay ready

After a request is sent, the guest needs confidence that it landed and a simple way to see what comes next.

  1. Queue view should show order, wait context, and request status at a glance.
  2. Hosts can remind guests that moderated requests may wait for approval before going fully live.
  3. Guests should keep the phone handy once they are near the top.

What the room staff should say out loud

"Scan the QR, join room AAHF, pick your emoji and name, clear the quick rules, then search your song. After you send it, watch the queue on your phone."

Operations View

What helpers and hosts do behind the scenes

The host and helper layers should feel coordinated, not duplicated. Helpers build momentum; the host confirms and steers the room.

Helper catalog

This is the approved co-host tool for long-ahead queue building. It is the fastest way to help guests without handing them a second host dashboard.

  1. Select the singer first, then search or browse the song.
  2. Add songs on behalf of guests when the room needs help moving faster.
  3. Use Tell Host only for useful signals about audio, pacing, or guest issues.
  4. Keep this tool limited to trusted helpers on approved devices.

Host workspace

This is where the night stays coherent: the live lane stays left, while add to queue, current queue, and run of show share one unified rail.

  1. Use the host workspace as the source of truth for timing and approvals.
  2. Switch between add, queue, and show from the same content rail.
  3. Promote trusted helpers from Audience: select the guest, tap Make Co-Host, and confirm the CO-HOST badge.
  4. Let helpers assist with adds and signals, but keep final control here.

Helper rule of thumb

Helpful, fast, and narrow. Add the right songs, protect pacing, and use Tell Host only when the signal actually helps the room.

Promote a co-host from Audience

  1. Open Audience.
  2. Click the guest in Lobby Lineup or on their audience card.
  3. Tap Make Co-Host.
  4. Look for the CO-HOST badge. Use Remove Co-Host to undo it.
